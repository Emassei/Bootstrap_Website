Self-Preservation

The 'physical' investigation is meant to disclose the precise character of the state of nature.

Man, is not by nature capable of the full use of his reason, i.e. he is not subject to perfect duties of natural law. I would say walking perfect by convention, supplied by reason alone.

Natural law, then must have its roots in principles which are anterior to reasion, i.e., in passions which need not be specfically human; self-preservation.

Which implies the right of each to be the sole judge of what are the proper means for his self-preservation.
