Unnatural Reason

Rosseau does not need to rely on experiements of the "savage man" rather he will use a "meditation on the first and most simple operations of the human soul."

Man is by nature asocial, pride or amour-propre presupposes society. Natural man cannot be proud or vain, thus pride or vanity are the root of all viciousness, natural man is fee from all viciousness!

Natural man, thus, would only harm another if it was for the sake of self-preservation, not for its own sake, as is the case with pride or vanity.
